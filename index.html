kahveci group research

Welcome to the Kahveci Group! At the Kahveci Group, we are passionate about advancing science education through innovative research, impactful teaching, and dedicated service. With a foundation built at Loyola University Chicago (LUC), our work continues to evolve, now encompassing current roles in both K-12 and higher education.

Research and Innovation

Our research lies at the intersection of education, technology, and psychology, with a focus on chemistry education. From understanding the affective states of teachers and students to exploring instructional interactivity and technology integration, we aim to transform science learning experiences. Highlights include:

  • Publications: Co-edited the book "Affective Dimensions in Chemistry Education" and authored impactful studies on instructional interactivity, alternative assessments, and international data analyses.
  • Assessment Tools: Development of graphical representations to improve student understanding of complex concepts.
  • International Collaboration: Contributions as an EU expert reviewer for FP7 Science in Society proposals and presentations at premier conferences such as the Biennial Conference on Chemical Education and the European Conference on Research in Chemical Education.

Teaching Excellence

As a Science Teacher at Chicago Public Schools, I focus on student-centered learning approaches, inquiry-based laboratory exercises, and the integration of the Skyline science curriculum. These strategies empower high school students to connect theoretical knowledge with practical applications, fostering a deep engagement with chemistry and biology.

In my role as an Adjunct Professor of Chemistry at Wheeling University, I deliver online instruction for CHEM 221 Organic Chemistry I. By leveraging virtual learning strategies, I strive to create an engaging, accessible environment for students to master foundational principles of organic chemistry.

Service and Community Engagement

Dedicated to supporting both the academic and local community, I focus on:

  • Mentorship: Preparing the next generation of educators and scientists by mentoring students and colleagues.
  • Curriculum Development: Designing and implementing inquiry-based and culturally responsive curricula aligned with educational standards.
  • Global Impact: Contributing to international science education reform through collaborative projects and knowledge dissemination.

The Kahveci Group continues to build on a legacy of integrating research and teaching to meet the needs of a diverse and evolving educational landscape. We are committed to advancing equitable science education and fostering a lifelong passion for learning in students at all levels.
