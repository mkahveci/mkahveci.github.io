Introduction: The Challenge The core challenge for educators is answering a critical question in real-time: **"Are my students learning what I am teaching, right now?"** * Traditional grade books are often too slow for effective formative assessment. * Collecting timely, actionable data can be an overwhelming administrative burden. * We need a streamlined, efficient, and mobile-friendly solution to bridge the gap between instruction and assessment. --- ## Introducing the LT Tracker (5-SC) âœ¨ A mobile-friendly Google Sheet template designed for educators to **efficiently monitor student progress** against specific learning targets. * **Goal:** To make informed, data-driven pedagogical decisions without the paperwork. * **Method:** A simple, reusable system for daily data entry and automated analysis. * **Outcome:** Leverage data and AI to plan targeted instruction and form dynamic cooperative learning groups. --- ## 2. The LT Tracker System: A Guided Tour Let's explore the structure and workflow of the tool. --- ## Core Concepts: LT & SC The system is built on two fundamental ideas: * **Learning Target (LT):** The overarching concept or skill students are expected to master. * *Example: "I can write a balanced chemical equation."* * **Success Criteria (SC):** The specific, observable skills that prove mastery of the LT. The tracker is designed for up to five SCs per LT. * *Example SCs: 1) Identify reactants & products. 2) Write correct chemical formulas. 3) Apply the law of conservation of mass.* --- ## Getting Started: Initial Setup Setting up a new tracker for a Learning Target is a simple, three-step process. 1. **Copy the Template:** In Google Drive, right-click the template file `[TEMPLATE] LT Tracker (5-SC)` and select **Make a copy**. Rename it for your specific LT (e.g., "LT1 Tracker - Chemical Reactions"). 2. **Populate the Roster:** Open the new file and go to the **Roster_Key** tab. Paste your class list, ensuring each student has a Name, a unique Student ID, and a Class Section. 3. **Define Success Criteria:** Go to the **SC-1_Entry** tab. In cell **A1**, replace the placeholder with your actual Success Criterion ID (e.g., `LT1-SC1`). Repeat for all other `SC-X_Entry` tabs. --- ## Daily Data Entry: A Mobile-First Workflow ðŸ“± This workflow is designed for speed and accuracy, allowing you to capture data live in the classroom. 1. Navigate to the correct tab for the skill you're assessing (e.g., `SC-2_Entry`). 2. Find the student you are observing. 3. In the `Performance Level` column, click the cell and select the appropriate level from the dropdown menu. **That's it!** The data is saved, and your dashboard is instantly updated. --- {% include slide-figure-text.html title="Mobile Data Entry" image_src="/images/blog/pab/mobile-data-entry.png" fig_num="1" caption_title="" caption_body="The tracker is designed for quick, on-the-go use, allowing for instant data entry while observing students." text_items="" image_align="right" %} --- ## 3. Ask for specific, actionable instructional strategies. --- ## Sample AI Prompt: Instructional Planning ```prompt You are an expert high school science instructional coach. [Paste your student data from the Progress Matrix here] --- ## AI-Driven Dynamic Grouping for Kagan Structures The same data can be used to form purposeful, dynamic cooperative learning groups.
